# DESIGN.md

## Purpose
This document reverse-engineers the visual style of the reference deck and turns it into a reusable design system for future AI-generated slide work.

This is an **inferred style spec**, not an exact extraction from the original source file.

---

## 1) Core Style Summary

**Style name:** Warm Editorial Watercolor Story Deck  
**Visual feeling:** trustworthy, educational, gentle, family-centered, inspirational  
**Primary use cases:** parenting, education, soft-skill training, community talks, emotional keynote, values-based presentations  

This deck style blends:
- watercolor children’s-book illustration
- magazine/editorial slide composition
- light academic presentation structure
- soft motivational storytelling

It is **not** a corporate enterprise deck, not futuristic neon SaaS, and not a data-heavy consulting template.

---

## 2) Visual DNA

### 2.1 Emotional tone
The deck communicates through reassurance, warmth, and clarity.

Use these emotional signals:
- nurturing
- hopeful
- calm
- human-centered
- family-safe
- reflective
- optimistic, but not flashy

Avoid:
- aggressive urgency
- dark drama
- cold minimalism
- techno-futurist overload
- over-serious academic stiffness

---

## 3) Composition Principles

### 3.1 Default slide anatomy
Most slides follow one of these structures:

#### A. Title + illustration hero
- Large title block on left or upper-left
- Main illustration occupying 40–65% of slide
- Very little extra text
- Used for cover, section openers, closing, gratitude slides

#### B. Title + short evidence block + illustration
- Title on top-left
- 1–3 key statements or a paragraph on left
- Illustration on right or lower-right
- Used for “science says”, explanatory or persuasive slides

#### C. Numbered tip slide
- Round number badge in top-left
- Big bold heading
- 2–4 short lines of guidance
- Related illustration on opposite side

#### D. Comparison / statistic slide
- Big numeric callouts
- Simple signpost or metaphorical image
- Very little charting
- Used when presenting evidence without dense graphs

### 3.2 Layout rhythm
The deck feels consistent because every slide keeps:
- generous whitespace
- stable margins
- one dominant focal area
- one dominant message per slide
- illustration anchored to one side, not floating randomly

### 3.3 Density rule
Text density is intentionally low.

Target:
- **Title:** 4–10 words, occasionally 2 lines
- **Body:** 20–60 words on normal content slides
- **Bullet list:** 3–6 bullets maximum
- **Citation line:** 1 short source line only

If the content feels crowded, split into more slides.

## 4) Typography System

### 4.1 Typography personality
Typography combines:
- bold geometric or rounded sans-serif for structure
- handwritten / brush / script accent for emotional emphasis

This contrast is central to the deck’s charm.

### 4.2 Functional roles

#### Role A — Main headings
Use a bold rounded sans-serif.
Visual behavior:
- uppercase or title case
- dark navy
- high legibility
- compact line height

Suggested font directions:
- Montserrat ExtraBold
- Nunito ExtraBold
- Baloo 2 SemiBold/Bold
- Quicksand Bold
- Poppins Bold

#### Role B — Accent words
Use handwritten/script only for emphasis.
Examples:
- “AI” on cover
- emotionally resonant phrase on hook slide
- “Cảm ơn!” closing slide

Suggested font directions:
- Pacifico
- Caveat Bold
- Merienda
- Great Vibes (use carefully)
- Dancing Script (only for short phrases)

Rules:
- script must never dominate paragraphs
- max 1–2 accent moments per slide
- do not use script for citations or bullets

#### Role C — Body text
Use clean sans-serif, medium weight.
Characteristics:
- friendly, readable, slightly rounded if possible
- dark gray or navy
- comfortable line spacing

Suggested font directions:
- Nunito
- Poppins
- Open Sans
- Source Sans 3
- Lato

### 4.3 Typographic scale
Approximate relative scale for 16:9 slide:
- Cover title: very large, 30–42 pt equivalent
- Section/title slide heading: 24–34 pt
- Body heading / numbered slide heading: 20–28 pt
- Body text: 12–18 pt
- Citation/source: 9–11 pt
- Footer credit: 8–10 pt

### 4.4 Text styling rules
- Headings: dark navy, bold, compact
- Accent text: watercolor-compatible script in purple/pink/soft blue
- Body: regular or medium weight
- Bullet markers: colored icons or checkmarks, not default black bullets when possible
- Never use harsh black for everything

## 5) Color System

### 5.1 Palette character
The palette is pastel watercolor with warm sunlight and soft sky tones.

### 5.2 Base palette
Approximate usable palette:
- **Navy title:** `#1F3A6D`
- **Soft blue:** `#8EC5E8`
- **Sky wash:** `#DDEFFD`
- **Warm yellow:** `#F6C64E`
- **Peach:** `#F7B28B`
- **Coral pink:** `#E9878D`
- **Lavender accent:** `#A786D8`
- **Grass green:** `#97C77A`
- **Warm beige paper:** `#FBF7F0`
- **Body text gray-navy:** `#334155`

### 5.3 Color usage ratios
Recommended balance:
- 60% soft neutrals / white / paper tones
- 25% light blues and sky washes
- 10% warm accents (yellow, coral, peach)
- 5% strong emphasis (navy, violet)

### 5.4 Color behavior
Use color to:
- separate title hierarchy
- support emotional tone
- highlight numbered steps
- make illustrations feel cohesive

Do not:
- flood slides with saturated color blocks
- use dark backgrounds for most slides
- use too many unrelated bright hues

## 6) Illustration Style

### 6.1 Illustration identity
The strongest style signal in the deck is the illustration approach.

Characteristics:
- watercolor / gouache feel
- soft brush edges
- storybook warmth
- expressive but simple faces
- family, child, home, nature, learning scenes
- painterly backgrounds with light detail
- emotionally readable silhouettes

### 6.2 Subject matter patterns
Common image types in the reference:
- parent and child bonding
- child learning or thinking
- family group portrait
- aspirational outdoor scene
- symbolic metaphor scene (stairs, road sign, lighthouse, path)

### 6.3 Illustration composition rules
- one clear primary subject group
- avoid cluttered micro-details
- leave negative space for text
- keep background airy and luminous
- illustrations should feel “gentle”, not hyper-realistic

### 6.4 What not to use
Avoid these image styles:
- photo-realistic stock photography
- glossy 3D renders
- flat corporate vectors
- anime with hard outlines
- dark cinematic fantasy
- sterile medical/office stock style

## 7) Shape Language

### 7.1 Number badges
The circular step badge is a recurring signature.

Badge style:
- solid filled circle
- warm accent color or pastel color
- white number centered
- placed near top-left or slide heading area
- subtle shadow optional

Suggested badge colors by section:
- yellow/orange
- coral
- teal
- pink
- blue
- green

### 7.2 Supporting shapes
Use sparingly:
- light blobs / watercolor washes
- small heart icons
- checkmarks
- doodle thought icons
- cloud-like soft outlines

Avoid rigid heavy boxes unless absolutely needed.

---

## 8) Background Treatment

### 8.1 Default background
Mostly white or warm off-white with very light watercolor wash.

### 8.2 Decorative treatment
Optional background effects:
- subtle blue sky wash at top corners
- soft painted texture behind illustration
- paper-like warmth
- faint nature gradient, never too strong

### 8.3 Constraint
Background must never compete with the title or illustration.

## 10) Content Style

### 10.1 Voice
The writing is:
- direct
- encouraging
- practical
- emotionally intelligent
- easy for non-expert audiences

### 10.2 Sentence pattern
Good slide copy in this style:
- short headline
- one idea per block
- evidence simplified into human language
- concrete action guidance

### 10.3 Citation treatment
Research is present, but softly.

Citation behavior:
- cite lightly under the claim
- use one source line, not footnote overload
- format should not dominate slide aesthetics

## 11) Slide Archetypes Library

### 11.1 Cover slide
Ingredients:
- strong main title
- one accent script word
- emotional family illustration
- little/no body text

### 11.2 Hook slide
Ingredients:
- provocative or urgent statement
- script emphasis line
- high-emotion child/future imagery

### 11.3 Pain-point slide
Ingredients:
- title framing concern/problem
- small bullets/icons
- caring parent-child visual

### 11.4 “Science says” slide
Ingredients:
- numbered evidence point
- one supporting paragraph
- one related illustration
- 1 citation line

### 11.5 Market/future slide
Ingredients:
- bold numeric callouts
- simple metaphorical illustration
- minimal text

### 11.6 Tip slide
Ingredients:
- number badge
- action-oriented title
- 2–4 lines of guidance
- human or symbolic illustration

### 11.7 Conclusion slide
Ingredients:
- short summary
- hopeful image
- calm emotional closure

### 11.8 Thank-you slide
Ingredients:
- large script gratitude phrase
- warm family/outdoor closing visual
- one line of emotional sign-off

---

## 12) Layout Guidelines for 16:9 Slides

### 12.1 Margins
Recommended safe area:
- left/right margins: 5–7% of width
- top/bottom margins: 5–7% of height

### 12.2 Content split
Common split ratios:
- 55/45 text-image
- 50/50 for balanced slides
- 40/60 on hero visual slides

### 12.3 Alignment
- Left-align almost all title/body copy
- Center only cover, thank-you, or special emotional slides
- Keep text blocks visually anchored

---

## 13) Motion and Transition Guidance

If turned into animated slides:
- use very soft fades
- gentle upward motion
- minimal parallax only
- no fast spinning, glitch, or hard zoom transitions

Animation tone should feel calm, storybook, and respectful.

---

## 14) Adaptation Rules

### 14.1 What can change
You may adapt:
- topic domain
- illustration subject
- section colors
- amount of evidence
- age group focus

### 14.2 What must remain stable
To preserve the style, keep:
- soft watercolor illustration language
- bold navy title structure
- low text density
- emotionally legible slide storytelling
- pastel and airy palette
- step-badge system for numbered content

---

## 15) Anti-Patterns

Do not let future AI generations do the following:
- cram paragraphs into slides
- replace illustration style with random stock images
- use heavy corporate templates or dark gradients
- overuse script fonts
- place text on top of busy illustration details
- introduce tables/charts unless absolutely necessary
- mix many unrelated visual styles in one deck

---

## 16) Reusable Prompt Cues for AI Image Generation

Use phrases like:
- warm watercolor children’s book illustration
- gentle editorial presentation art
- soft pastel family scene
- airy sky, glowing sunlight, emotional storytelling
- painterly brush texture, clean negative space for slide text
- wholesome educational keynote visual

Avoid prompt phrases like:
- photorealistic stock photo
- ultra-detailed sci-fi render
- neon cyberpunk
- corporate office vector
- glossy 3D infographic

---

## 17) Success Criteria
A new slide fits this style if it feels like:
- a caring keynote, not a sales pitch
- illustrated editorial storytelling, not stock design
- soft but structured
- research-backed but emotionally human
- polished enough for public speaking, simple enough for parents/teachers
